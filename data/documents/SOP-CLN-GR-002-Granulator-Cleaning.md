# SOP-CLN-GR-002 · High-Shear Granulator — Cleaning Procedure

**Document ID:** SOP-CLN-GR-002  
**Version:** 2.0  
**Effective Date:** 2025-04-01  
**Next Review:** 2026-04-01  
**Owner:** Manufacturing Operations  
**Equipment Applies To:** Glatt GPCG-60 (GR-204), and equivalent high-shear granulators  
**Associated SOPs:** SOP-MAN-GR-001, SOP-DEV-001, SOP-QA-001

---

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the cleaning procedure for the Glatt GPCG-60 High-Shear Granulator (GR-204) to prevent cross-contamination between product campaigns and to maintain GMP compliance per EU GMP Annex 15 §12 (cleaning validation) and 21 CFR Part 211.67.

---

## 2. Scope

Applies to all cleaning activities on GR-204 following:
- End of a product campaign (campaign clean)
- Planned maintenance or equipment quarantine
- Deviation events requiring equipment investigation

---

## 3. Cleaning Types

| Clean Type | Trigger | Validated Method | Residue Limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Campaign clean** | After each batch of same product | Aqueous wash + WFI rinse | ≤10 ppm API |
| **Product changeover clean** | Change to different product | Extended wash + WFI rinse × 2 | ≤10 ppm API; ≤1 ppm previous API |
| **Deep clean** | After equipment quarantine or bearing replacement | Full disassembly + extended wash | Validated residue limits |

---

## 4. Materials Required

- Purified Water (PW) — minimum 60°C
- Water for Injection (WFI) — final rinse
- Cleaning agent: 2% v/v approved detergent (see approved supplier list)
- Clean-room grade non-shedding cleaning cloths
- pH meter (calibrated)
- Conductivity meter (calibrated, WFI rinse verification)
- Swab test kit (for equipment cleaned after deviation events or quarantine)

---

## 5. Cleaning Procedure

### 5.1 Pre-Cleaning Safety

1. Confirm GR-204 is powered off and locked out (LOTO procedure).
2. No product or batch materials remain in the bowl.
3. Wear appropriate PPE (gown, gloves, eye protection).

### 5.2 Gross Cleaning

1. Remove impeller assembly.
2. Remove chopper blade assembly.
3. Remove spray nozzle and tubing.
4. Manually remove visible product residues from bowl, impeller housing, and chopper housing using dry cleaning cloth.

### 5.3 Aqueous Wash Cycle

1. Charge bowl with 10 liters of purified water (≥60°C).
2. Add cleaning agent at 2% v/v concentration.
3. Run impeller at 100 RPM for 5 minutes.
4. Drain cleaning solution.
5. **Repeat** wash cycle once more for product changeover clean or deviation-related clean.

### 5.4 Rinse Cycle

1. Charge bowl with 10 liters of WFI.
2. Run impeller at 100 RPM for 3 minutes.
3. Collect final rinse sample (100 mL, last fraction).
4. **Verify rinse water:** pH 6.0–7.5, conductivity ≤1.3 µS/cm. Document in cleaning record.
5. If pH or conductivity out of range: repeat rinse and retest.

### 5.5 Manual Clean — Impeller, Chopper, Spray Nozzle

1. Scrub all disassembled components with cleaning solution and non-shedding cloth.
2. Rinse each component with WFI (minimum 3 rinses).
3. Inspect all surfaces for residues — visual inspection under 500 lux.
4. For product changeover or deviation clean: swab 5 designated locations (impeller face, bowl wall, spray nozzle inner, chopper blade, discharge port) per swab procedure.

### 5.6 Drying

1. After cleaning, run inlet air system at maximum flow for 15 minutes to air-dry interior surfaces.
2. Wipe stainless steel surfaces with dry WFI-rinsed cloth.
3. Visual inspection: no water droplets, no residues, no lint.

---

## 6. Cleaning Verification and Release

1. IPQA officer performs independent visual inspection.
2. Review analytical results (rinse pH, conductivity; swab results if applicable).
3. Swab result acceptance: ≤10 ppm API equivalent (product campaign) or ≤1 ppm previous API (changeover). Per validated cleaning limits.
4. Complete cleaning logbook entry: date, time, operator, clean type, analytical results.
5. Apply **"CLEAN — Ready for Use"** label to equipment.

> **GMP Note:** Equipment shall not be used for production unless cleaning logbook entry is complete and "Clean" label is affixed. Missing cleaning record constitutes a documentation deviation per SOP-DEV-001.

---

## 7. Maximum Hold Time After Cleaning

| Equipment State | Maximum Hold Time |
|---|---|
| Cleaned and closed (lid sealed) | 7 days |
| Cleaned and open to environment | 24 hours |

If hold time is exceeded, repeat §5.3–5.4 (rinse cycle) before use. Document in BPR.

---

## 8. Deviation During Cleaning

If during cleaning inspection:
- Visible API residues remain after 2 wash cycles → raise deviation per SOP-DEV-001
- Equipment damage discovered (cracks, corrosion, worn seals) → quarantine GR-204, raise CMMS work order
- Swab result exceeds acceptance limit → cleaning failure deviation, root cause investigation required
