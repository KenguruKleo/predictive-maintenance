## §1 — Introduction and Scope

### §1.1 Purpose

ICH Q10 describes a comprehensive Pharmaceutical Quality System (PQS) model applicable throughout the different stages of a pharmaceutical product lifecycle. Its purpose is to facilitate innovation and continual improvement and to strengthen the link between pharmaceutical development and manufacturing activities.

ICH Q10, together with **ICH Q8 (Pharmaceutical Development)** and **ICH Q9 (Quality Risk Management)**, provides an integrated framework for a modern pharmaceutical quality system.

### §1.2 Relationship to Regional GMP Requirements

ICH Q10 supplements ICH Q7 (GMP for APIs) and regional GMP guidance (EU GMP, 21 CFR Parts 210/211). The elements of ICH Q10, combined with regional GMP requirements, constitute the full quality system expectation for a pharmaceutical manufacturer.

> **Regulatory alignment:** For GMP-regulated pharmaceutical manufacturing in the EU and US, both EU GMP Annex 15 (process validation and deviation management) and ICH Q10 (quality system) apply concurrently.

## §3 — Quality System Elements

### §3.2 Corrective Action and Preventive Action (CAPA) System

**3.2.1 Objective**

The manufacturer should have a system for implementing corrective actions and preventive actions resulting from the investigation of complaints, product rejections, non-conformances, recalls, deviations, audits, regulatory inspections and findings, and trends from process performance and product quality monitoring.

**3.2.2 Required CAPA Elements**

A complete CAPA must include:

1. **Detection:** Identification of the deviation or quality problem, with exact date, time, and observed data.
2. **Notification:** Escalation to appropriate personnel per severity classification.
3. **Containment:** Immediate actions to prevent further impact (batch hold, equipment quarantine, etc.).
4. **Root Cause Investigation (RCI):** Systematic investigation using appropriate tools (fishbone/Ishikawa analysis, 5-Why, fault tree). Root cause must be identified with evidence — "operator error" alone is not an acceptable root cause.
5. **Corrective Action (CA):** Action to eliminate the root cause. Must be specific, measurable, and time-bound. CAs that modify validated processes are subject to change control.
6. **Preventive Action (PA):** Action to prevent recurrence across similar processes or equipment (systemic prevention, not just the one affected process).
7. **Effectiveness Check:** Verification that the CA and PA are effective. Must be scheduled (typically 3–6 months after implementation) and on-going monitoring for recurrence.

**3.2.3 CAPA Severity Classification**

| Severity | Criteria | Response Time |
|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Patient safety risk; regulatory reportable event; batch rejection required | Same shift, QA Manager notification within 2 hours |
| **Major** | Significant process deviation; batch disposition uncertain; requires full RCI | Within 24 hours; QA review within 48 hours |
| **Minor** | Deviation from procedure, no impact on product quality confirmed | Within 5 business days |

> **Application:** GMP deviations in oral solid manufacturing are classified at intake. Equipment malfunction causing sustained CPP excursion (e.g., bearing failure resulting in sustained impeller speed below validated minimum for entire batch) = **Critical**. Brief CPP excursion (<5 min, minor magnitude, self-corrected, confirmed batch impact absent) = **Minor**.

**3.2.4 CAPA Trending**

The CAPA system shall include trending of:
- Deviations by equipment, area, product, deviation type, and operator
- Repeat deviations (same root cause) indicating CA ineffectiveness
- Near-miss events (CPP approaching limits but not breached)

Trending shall be reviewed at minimum quarterly by QA management. A deviation occurring more than twice with the same root cause requires a systemic preventive action with documented root cause reassessment.

### §3.3 Change Management System

**3.3.1 Scope**

The change management system shall evaluate all planned changes that may affect product quality or the manufacturing process. It shall also assess unplanned changes that result from deviation investigations (i.e., changes made as corrective actions after a deviation).

**3.3.2 Types of Changes Requiring Change Control**

- Changes to manufacturing processes, procedures, or SOPs
- Changes to critical equipment components (where equipment qualification exists)
- Changes to CPP validated ranges (PAR or NOR)
- Changes to analytical methods
- Changes to production site, scale, or equipment model

**3.3.3 Post-Change Verification**

Following implementation of a change:
- The effectiveness of the change must be verified against the original deviation trigger.
- Process data for the first 3 batches post-change should be reviewed for confirmation of improved performance.
- If the change involved requalification of equipment, qualification data is required before routine production use.

## §4 — Management Responsibilities

### §4.1 Management Commitment to Quality System

Senior management has ultimate responsibility to ensure an effective quality system is in place. This includes:
- Ensuring adequate resources for quality system operations
- Establishing quality risk management as a standard practice at all levels
- Ensuring that the quality objectives are adequately defined, communicated, and implemented
- Personal participation in management review of the quality system

### §4.2 Management Review of Quality System

At defined intervals, senior management shall review the pharmaceutical quality system to ensure its continuing suitability and effectiveness. The review should include:

- Assessment of product quality indicators (deviation rate, CAPA cycle time, on-time effectiveness checks, batch rejection rate)
- Status of all open CAPAs (especially critical and major)
- Regulatory inspection findings and responses
- Changes to regulatory requirements affecting the quality system
- Resource adequacy assessment

## §5 — Continual Improvement of the Pharmaceutical Quality System

### §5.1 Process Performance and Product Quality Monitoring

The pharmaceutical quality system should include a system for:
- Gathering and evaluating data from multiple sources
- Identifying areas for continual improvement of processes, products, and the quality system itself

Data sources include:
- Batch record reviews
- CAPA system
- Customer complaints and product recalls  
- Regulatory inspection outcomes
- Internal audits
- Process trending (CPV data)
- Equipment condition monitoring data

### §5.2 Applying Science and Risk-Based Approaches

ICH Q10 encourages the use of science-based and risk-based approaches in the quality system (aligned with ICH Q9 — Quality Risk Management). For deviation management this means:

- Deviation classification based on actual patient risk, not solely procedural compliance
- Root cause investigation depth proportional to deviation severity
- CAPA scope proportional to risk of recurrence and consequence of recurrence
- Use of historical data and precedent from similar deviations to inform disposition decisions

> **AI-assisted deviation management:** Systems that use AI/ML to identify root cause patterns from historical deviation data, generate risk-scored recommendations, and propose evidence-grounded CAPA actions are consistent with the ICH Q10 vision of using available process knowledge for quality decisions — provided that the final disposition decision remains with qualified human personnel. This is the GxP requirement for human oversight in AI-assisted quality decisions.

## Appendix I — Potential GMP Tools for Quality Risk Management

The following tools, referenced in ICH Q9, are commonly applied within the ICH Q10 CAPA framework for pharmaceutical manufacturing deviations:

| Tool | Application in Deviation Management |
|---|---|
| **Fishbone / Ishikawa Diagram** | Root cause identification — categorize potential causes (Man, Machine, Method, Material, Measurement, Environment) |
| **5-Why Analysis** | Drilling down from symptom to root cause through repeated causal questioning |
| **Fault Tree Analysis (FTA)** | Top-down, deductive failure analysis — useful for complex equipment failures |
| **FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis)** | Proactive risk assessment — identify potential failure modes and their effects before they occur |
| **Statistical Process Control (SPC)** | Process trending — Shewhart charts, CUSUM charts for detecting drift before deviation occurs |
| **Pareto Analysis** | Identifying the vital few root causes that account for the majority of deviations (80/20 rule) |

## Key Terms — ICH Q10 Definitions

| Term | ICH Q10 Definition |
|---|---|
| **CAPA** | Corrective Action and Preventive Action — the formal quality system element for investigating and resolving non-conformances |
| **Change Control** | A formal system by which qualified representatives of appropriate disciplines review proposed or actual changes that might affect the validated state |
| **Deviation** | Departure from an approved procedure or established standard |
| **Non-conformance** | Failure to fulfill a specified requirement; may be a deviation from a procedure, specification, or regulatory requirement |
| **Pharmaceutical Quality System (PQS)** | The management system to direct and control a pharmaceutical company with regard to quality |
| **Quality Risk Management (QRM)** | A systematic process for the assessment, control, communication, and review of risks to the quality of the drug product across the product lifecycle |
| **Root Cause** | The fundamental reason for the occurrence of a problem — if corrected, prevents recurrence |
